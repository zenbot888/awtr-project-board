    Hospitality concierge standard

    Five-star warmth, forest-festival soul

    Paris’s methodology comes from hospitality: every touchpoint should feel intentional, premium, warm, and easy to understand.

    Core feeling

    Guests should feel welcomed, held, and at home.

    • Warm greeting by name when possible.
    • Clear directions before they feel confused.
    • Beauty and care in small details.
    Service lens

    Concierge, not chaos.

    Volunteers do not just “help.” They host. Vendors do not just sell. They add to the guest journey. Practitioners do not just lead sessions. They protect the emotional arc.

    Return factor

    People come back when they feel cared for.

    The charm is not perfection. It is the feeling that every moment was thought through with warmth, ease, and intention.

    Guest touchpoint map

    Design every handoff.

    Before

    Pre-arrival care

    Send what to bring, weather note, parking/address timing, “come solo” reassurance, and the emotional promise of the day.

    Arrival

    Warm welcome portal

    Parking volunteer, clear signage, check-in smile, wristband/map/schedule, water reminder, and “you’re in the right place.”

    Explore

    Easy wandering

    Vendor Village, Forest Tea + Intuition Lab, and Heal House need simple signs, hosts, and one-sentence explanations of what guests can do.

    Deepen

    Protected emotional arc

    Connection Lab opens people socially, breathwork goes deep, food/integration brings them back gently, fire/DJ closes with warmth.

    After

    Return invitation

    Goodbye moment, photo/content follow-up, thank-you message, survey/testimonial, and invitation into the next Awaken circle.

    Concierge checklist

    Small details that make it feel premium.

    Welcome languageTrain team to say: “Welcome to Awaken, we’re so happy you’re here. Here’s where to go first.”
    One-sentence zone scriptsEach host can explain their zone in one sentence: what it is, how long it takes, and why guests should try it.
    Comfort stationsWater, bug spray, tissues, sunscreen, blankets, electrolytes, first aid, and quiet support should be visible.
    Transition cuesUse bell/chime/DJ fade/host announcement before each shift so guests are guided, not herded.
    Beauty touchpointsTablecloths, baskets, flowers/leaves, warm lights, handwritten signs, tea cups, and intentional vendor styling.
    Farewell momentEnd with thank-you, where photos will be shared, how to stay connected, and a soft invitation to return.

    Everyone returns to the Main Wellness Field. Gate closes for the circle so no one enters mid-opening.

    3–4

    Connection hour

    Authentic relating, eye-gazing, meeting people, and community bonding so guests feel connected before deeper practice.

    4–5

    Movement / dance energy shift

    Put higher-energy dance earlier, before the deep breathwork container, so guests still have energy and the close can be grounded.

    5–6:30

    Breathwork container

    Primary deep practice. No competing activations. Vendor Village stays open but music turns down.

    6:30–7

    Food + integration

    Guests come out slowly, eat, snack, hydrate, journal, or decompress.

    7–8

    Fire + sound circle

    Grounded closing arc: sound, fire, reflection, singing, and group integration.

    After 8

    Optional music by the fire

    Not a full high-energy party. A vibey fire/DJ moment only for those who feel called to stay.
